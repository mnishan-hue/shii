A letter for you, My dearest Shiyaa,

Many many happy returns of the day! 🤍

You know what's funny to think about now? We were in each other's lives for years before we were actually friends. Our parents knew each other, we knew each other, but it took your semester holidays and one very loud, very dramatic game of UNO to change everything. You, me, and mommies all crammed around that table, laughing and peeping to see others cards and acting like winning meant everything. Something about those evenings just felt so warm. And quietly, without either of us really planning it, we became close. I'm so glad we did.

After that came the random texts. The "hey are you okay" messages out of nowhere. Telling each other things we wouldn't really tell most people. The small fights that somehow never lasted more than five minutes because neither of us could stay annoyed at the other for long as we laughed like ediots on each others jokes. And Bangalore, you, me and my brother (PN which you used to call him) we at the trampoline park, jumping around like absolute mokeys. and the craziest roll and pasta from paris panini..... still smile when I think about that day.

I just want to stop here and say something I don't think I've ever said out loud.

"I'm really glad you're my friend."

Not in the polite way people say it. I mean it in the way where I genuinely cannot imagine this past year without you in it. You're one of those rare people who makes everything feel a little warmer just by being around. The way you get excited about things, the way you care so deeply, the way you show up even when you're going through something yourself. That's just who you are, Shiyaa.

I know there are things you've carried quietly this year. Excitements that didn't go the way you hoped, disappointments you probably didn't even fully say out loud to anyone. I saw some of it and I know there's more I didn't see. But I want you to know, every single one of those silent things, every quiet ache, every moment you just swallowed and moved on from, it's not forgotten. Not by me, and not by Allah. Every bit of it is going somewhere beautiful. I really believe that for you.

I pray your dream of studying abroad comes true exactly the way you've imagined it. I pray every single thing you've whispered to yourself in hopeful moments finds its way back to you. And I have this picture in my head of your future that makes me so happy just thinking about it. You, somewhere near the beach, your dogs running chaotic circles around you, a coffee in your hand, the sun doing something golden in the background, and you just at peace. Genuinely, deeply happy. Not performing it. Actually feeling it. That's what I want for you more than anything.

And whatever life looks like for both of us going forward, I want you to know this friendship means everything to me. The UNO nights, the random texts, the small fights, the Bangalore memories, all of it. I don't take any of it for granted.

This year, more than anything, I just want to see our Shiyaa back. The one who walks in with that energy, who starts conversations with a "heyuu" out of nowhere, who is just fully, unapologetically happy. All those silent pains going completely vain. All that weight lifted. Just you, back to you, the way you're meant to be.

You will always, always have a place in my dua. On the good days and on the ones that are harder to get through. I'm rooting for you in this life more than you probably know.

Happy birthday, Shiyaa. The world is genuinely better with you in it. 🤍

— your Lil Bud
